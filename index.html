A community for formal mathematics · New York City

We meet to prove
things by hand,
and by machine.

NYC Lean is a group of mathematicians, programmers, and students in New York who gather to learn the Lean theorem prover, formalize mathematics, and build things together. Everyone is welcome — from the curious to the seasoned.

Come to a meetup See our projects ↗

01 — What we do

Learn Lean

Whether you've never written a proof or you maintain Mathlib, we work through ideas together — tactics, type theory, and the parts that only click out loud.

Formalize math

We turn theorems and textbooks into machine-checked proofs, and contribute the reusable pieces back to the broader Lean ecosystem.

Build & share

Members present what they're working on — tools, talks, and side projects — so everyone leaves having learned something new.

02 — Meetups

We get together most weekends, somewhere in the city.

Meetings move around New York — libraries, universities, and the occasional coffee shop. Each week someone leads with a short talk or a problem to work through, then we hack and chat.

The fastest way to find the next meetup is our chat and mailing list below — that's where times and locations get posted.

  • WhenMost weekends
  • WhereAround NYC — libraries & campuses
  • WhoAll levels, all backgrounds
  • CostFree — just show up

03 — What we're building

A Lean textbook companion

A community effort to write a Lean/Verso companion to a classic open-access textbook — formalized in a natural, pedagogical style so it reads as a true companion to the text. A great on-ramp at any skill level.
