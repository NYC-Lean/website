Formalizing the GNS Construction in Lean

A talk by Gregory Wickham.

This week we're hosting our first official NYC Lean talk, kicking things off with Gregory Wickham on his formalization of the Gelfand–Naimark–Segal (GNS) construction in Lean. From his abstract:

I formalized the Gelfand–Naimark–Segal (GNS) construction using the proof assistant Lean. The GNS construction is a method for constructing a Hilbert space and a ∗-homomorphism from a C∗-algebra into the algebra of bounded operators on that Hilbert space. The GNS construction is an essential step in the proof the Gelfand–Naimark theorem, which is a foundational theorem in the theory of C∗-algebras. This formalization has been “peer-reviewed” in the sense in which Lean formalization projects are reviewed, meaning that it has been merged into Mathlib, the canonical, open-source library of mathematics in Lean.

— Gregory Wickham, Formalizing the Gelfand–Naimark–Segal Construction in Lean

Its reach extends into physics, too: the construction and its generalizations are a standard tool in algebraic quantum field theory.

Algebraic Quantum Field Theory (AQFT) — the most successful attempt to reformulate QFT axiomatically — employs only bounded operators. It builds upon work in the 1940s by Gelfand, Neumark, and in particular Segal, who tried to describe quantum physics in terms of C∗-algebras.

— Quantum Field Theory, Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy

The construction now lives in Mathlib's C∗-algebra library, and getting it there was a collaborative effort: Gregory credits fellow Lean community members Jireh Loreaux and Monica Omar for their extensive feedback and contributions.

The mathematics we'll save for the talk — expect a tour of the formalization itself: the design decisions and the dead ends behind the proof. Gregory did the work as his senior thesis at Harvey Mudd College, advised by Lucas Bang; the full thesis and conference poster are below.
