Formalizing the GNS Construction in Lean

A talk by Gregory Wickham.

This month we're joined by Gregory Wickham, who will walk us through his formalization of the Gelfand–Naimark–Segal (GNS) construction in Lean.

The GNS construction is a method for building a Hilbert space and a C*-algebra *-homomorphism from that algebra into the bounded operators on the Hilbert space. It is an essential step in the proof of the Gelfand–Naimark theorem, a foundational result in the theory of C*-algebras that says every such algebra can be represented concretely as operators on a Hilbert space.

The formalization has been “peer-reviewed” in the sense that Lean formalization projects are reviewed: it has been merged into Mathlib, the canonical, open-source library of mathematics in Lean. The talk traces the path from the mathematics to the formal proof — lifting continuous semilinear maps, constructing the Hilbert space, and building the *-homomorphism — along with the design decisions and dead ends along the way.

Gregory completed this work as his senior thesis in the Department of Mathematics at Harvey Mudd College, advised by Lucas Bang with Michael Orrison as reader. The full thesis and conference poster are below.
