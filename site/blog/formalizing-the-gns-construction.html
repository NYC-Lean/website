Formalizing the GNS Construction in Lean

A talk by Gregory Wickham.

June 6, 2026

Tomorrow we're hosting our first official NYC Lean talk, kicking things off with Gregory Wickham on his formalization of the Gelfand–Naimark–Segal (GNS) construction. From his abstract:

I formalized the Gelfand–Naimark–Segal (GNS) construction using the proof assistant Lean. The GNS construction is a method for constructing a Hilbert space and a ∗-homomorphism from a C∗-algebra into the algebra of bounded operators on that Hilbert space. The GNS construction is an essential step in the proof the Gelfand–Naimark theorem, which is a foundational theorem in the theory of C∗-algebras. This formalization has been “peer-reviewed” in the sense in which Lean formalization projects are reviewed, meaning that it has been merged into Mathlib, the canonical, open-source library of mathematics in Lean.

— Gregory Wickham, Formalizing the Gelfand–Naimark–Segal Construction in Lean

Wickham's work formalizes a deep functional analysis result about the representation theory of infinite-dimensional complex vector spaces. Specifically, he studies a C∗-algebra A — a complex Banach space equipped with a ring structure and a “star” operation, akin to complex conjugation. The GNS construction then establishes a correspondence between the positive linear functionals on A (which live in its dual space) and the ∗-homomorphisms from A into the bounded operators on a Hilbert space. These ideas play an important role in mathematical physics:

Algebraic Quantum Field Theory (AQFT) — the most successful attempt to reformulate QFT axiomatically — employs only bounded operators. It builds upon work in the 1940s by Gelfand, Neumark, and in particular Segal, who tried to describe quantum physics in terms of C∗-algebras.

— Quantum Field Theory, Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy

This was a collaborative work with fellow Lean community members Jireh Loreaux and Monica Omar, who provided extensive feedback and contributions.

For more details, you'll have to come to Wickham's talk tomorrow at Tower 49, or watch the video on our YouTube channel. Gregory did the work as his senior thesis at Harvey Mudd College, advised by Lucas Bang; the full thesis and conference poster are below.
